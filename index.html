Nero Nodes

After DijxCloud, I moved on to Nero Nodes - a free hosting platform with powerful nodes and a great community.

After working at Nero for a while, I was promoted into an executive and ownership position.

At the age of 15, I have been able to improve and maintain a company with 1500+ servers, 2000+ clients and a reliable infrastructure.

In this position, I currently use management skills which include finances, staff, infrastructure and customer support.

We use technology such as Docker, Linux, nginx, PHP, JavaScript, TypeScript and more to make our infrastructure reliable.

I've also built a Disaster Recovery system which utilises the Vultr API to spin up a temporary instance and start servers on there if a main node goes offline.

This process involves sending API calls, using libraries such as Cloudflare-Node and Vultr-Node, and managing servers and backup systems.


Nero Nodes hit 2000 clients on 8th June 2021.


DijxCloud

One of my main projects recently was a startup called DijxCloud, a small hosting company specialising in Minecraft servers and development hosting.

We used tools such as Laravel, Docker, WHMCS, NGINX, Apache and Linux to help us run our core systems. All our client's servers ran in isolated containers ran with Docker and were accessible via our custom control panel, built on PteroPlus.

We built a community of over 100 people in our Discord Server and gained multiple clients who reviewed our services on TrustPilot.

My team of ~8 people and I worked day and night to ensure the highest quality service to our clients as a small hosting provider. One of the key steps we made was to use dedicated servers and an advanced billing portal to make our platform as good as possible.

We grew for approximately 6 months, reaching a peak profit of $100/month, before I later shut the project.

DijxCloud officially closed on 19 May, 2021.
